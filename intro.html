To: The Executive Chairmen of Local Government Areas (LGAs) & LCDAs, Nigeria

Dear Honorable Chairmen,

On behalf of **A-Plus Resources Ltd**, in strategic partnership with the **Greater Manchester Chamber of Commerce (GMCC)**—the largest accredited Chamber of Commerce in the United Kingdom—we have the privilege of inviting you to participate in this exclusive, high-impact bilateral executive development study programme.

This programme is specifically structured to address the core challenges faced by municipal administrators in Nigeria today. This programme focuses on **pragmatic, low-tech, and high-impact solutions** that can be directly implemented at the local government level to drive tangible development.

Greater Manchester provides the world's premier model for localized development. Over the last three decades, this industrial region devolved power to its ten local authority municipal councils, allowing them to coordinate infrastructure, manage waste-to-value streams, and build dynamic, self-sufficient local economies. This programme is designed to bypass abstract academic theories and deliver immediate, functional value. By participating, you will be equipped with the precise **knowledge, practical management tools, and direct access to UK-based investor and development networks** required to:

1. Deploy Leak-Proof IGR Models

Implement structured, step-by-step revenue collection models and manual-to-digital audit frameworks to capture informal sector levies cleanly and eliminate leakage at the source (avoiding GIGO failures).

2. Establish Waste-to-Value Models

Acquire off-the-shelf operational blueprints and sanitation models that transform organic market waste into commercial agricultural compost, creating local jobs.

3. Secure Alternative Capital Channels

Connect directly with UK-based municipal developers, public-private partnership (PPP) experts, and development finance networks to co-fund markets and motor parks.

4. Implement Asset Longevity Models

Equip engineering teams with simple, manual preventative maintenance schedules to triple the lifespan of community links and health clinics.

We look forward to partnering with your council as we build sustainable grassroots administrations across Nigeria.

Section 01: Strategic Intent

Bilateral Exchange & Practical Adaptation

The core philosophy of this programme is **bilateral exchange and practical adaptation**. Rather than presenting abstract theoretical concepts, this study tour focusing on **"Local Governance Efficiency"** will expose delegates to functioning modern municipal models with direct insights on how to adapt these models to the Nigerian context.

01

Devolved Governance Models

Understand how 10 independent local councils coordinate under a single unified authority (**Greater Manchester Combined Authority - GMCA**) to pool resources for joint developmental projects while completely retaining unique local council autonomy.

02

Alternative Infrastructure Financing

Learn how to attract and manage Public-Private Partnerships (PPPs) and generate robust Internally Generated Revenue (IGR) at the grassroots level using reliable collection models without over-relying on federal monthly allocations.

03

Practical Sanitation & Waste-to-Value Models

Explore the deployment of cost-effective, low-tech waste management programs, localized organic recycling, and market sanitation models that promote clean public areas and generate local jobs.

04

Strategic Public Leadership

Gain actionable frameworks for high-impact decision-making, transparent financial management, municipal project delivery, and community policy sustainability.

Core Program Focus Areas
Core Focus Area Practical Scope & Implementation
1. Fiscal Autonomy Expanding local tax bases, registering informal businesses, and digitalizing IGR.
2. Municipal Sanitation Low-tech waste-to-value, organized local dumpsites, and community cleaning jobs.
3. Community Infrastructure Cost-effective road maintenance, drainage management, and market facility upgrades.

Section 03: Alignment Blueprint

Strategic Collaboration & Development Framework

The delegation is built around permanent, ongoing development. In close cooperation with the GMCC, we have outlined **seven key strategic alignment tracks** designed to move participants from basic theory to long-term funding execution. This bilateral capacity development initiative builds upon and expands the structural frameworks proposed in the document **UK Event .pdf**, tailoring them directly to the needs of the Association of Local Governments of Nigeria (ALGON).

1. Direct Municipal Development Finance & Grants

Connecting local councils with UK international development funds (such as the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office - FCDO, and British International Investment - BII) to support sub-national basic infrastructural services without central reliance.

2. Grant Attraction & Capacity Building

Satisfying strict institutional donor requirements for accountability. Setting up robust anti-GIGO tracking models, audit trails, and financial tracking structures directly within your councils.

3. Live Proposal & Bid-Drafting Workshop

A non-theoretical workspace during Day 2 and Day 3 where delegates draft actual project outlines and logical framework concept notes under GMCC expert supervision.

4. A-Plus Post-Training Implementation Desk

Post-training implementation support in Nigeria. Our expert advisory team actively collaborates with returned councils to refine, formalize, and submit their proposal notes.

5. Simple Green Bond Models

Demystifying eco-financing. Training local authorities on how to pool environmental sanitation and waste-to-value programs to issue structured sub-national green notes.

6. Ongoing Chamber Affiliation & Sister-Council Twinning

Providing continuing value via special Associate Memberships with GMCC. We will explore pilot twinning linkages between selected Nigerian councils and Manchester boroughs.

7. Bilateral Protocol, Plaque Exchange & Souvenirs

Acknowledging traditional governance protocols. The program includes structured award ceremonies, bilateral plaque exchange, and hand-delivered symbolic host country souvenirs.
